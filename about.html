FAQ

Camp Site is a sleepaway camp in the mountians for boys aged 8 thorugh 18. We are ACA accredited which you can find out more about over here. Camp is two sessions, each about a month. Be sure to check the calendar for this years dates as well as any upcoming events.
We are located upstate New York in a friendly town called Monticello. Check out the map below for more info.
Don't you worry! Every kid is nervous the first time he goes away from home, even the one's who are too cool to admit it. Our staff is experienced and will be there to comfort your child if he gets homesick. We have a phone room where campers are allowed to call home every few days, so don't worry if you start to miss him too much!
Here at Camp Site we have a state of the art infirmary and top notch nurses. The infirmary is always open if your child is feeling under the weather and to give out medications. Counselors are aware of which of theor campers need medication and will make sure that they always go to take it.
Oh boy! What aren't they like. We have everything a kid could dream about for a summer vacation. Baseball fields, hockey rinks, baskball courts, football fields, soccer pitches, volleyball courts and that's just the sports! We have archery, a ropes course, rocketry, wilderness and more for the adventurers. When it's time to cool off campers can enjoy a swim in our newly renovated pool or check out the lake where we have kayaking, boating and a mini water park. And those are just the regular actvities! Every night we have special night activity from magicians to concerts to intercamp baseball games and more!
We have one visiting day for each session. You can come in starting at 10 AM. The camp is open for everyone on visiting day so you can spend the day joining in on your child's fun. If you want, you can also take him out of camp. Go by to the office to check him out and be sure to bring him back by 8PM or he'll miss out on a super special night activity. Be sure to check out the calendar for this summer's visiting day dates.
Don't worry, sending your child to camp for the first time can be quite frightening. Feel free to reach out to us for any questions. Our contact info can be found below.
